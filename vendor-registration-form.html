12. BERKELEY'S SUPPLIERS / SUBCONTRACTORS CODE OF CONDUCT

1. Introduction

Berkeley Services UAE LLC ("Berkeley Services") is dedicated to upholding the highest standards of ethical business practices, sustainability, and compliance in all its operations. This Vendor/Contractor Code of Conduct ("Code of Conduct") sets forth the principles and standards that all vendors, suppliers, and contractors must adhere to when conducting business with or on behalf of Berkeley Services.

By entering into a business relationship with Berkeley Services, vendors / subcontractors agree to comply with these standards as a fundamental condition of their engagement. Our goal is to promote sustainable growth, ethical conduct, and positive environmental impact within the Facilities Management (FM) industry.

2. Compliance with Laws and Regulations

Vendors / contractors are required to:

  • Comply with all applicable local and international laws, regulations, and standards, particularly those governing labour practices, environmental protection, and fair trade in the UAE.
  • Adhere to the Dubai Green Building Regulations and the UAE's Federal Law on Environmental Protection.
  • Obtain and maintain all necessary licenses, permits, and approvals to operate legally in the UAE.

3. Business Integrity and Ethics

  • Anti-Corruption and Anti-Bribery: Berkeley Services enforces a strict zero-tolerance policy against bribery and corruption. Vendors must not engage in any activities involving bribery, fraud, kickbacks, or other unethical practices.
  • Conflict of Interest: Vendors should avoid any activities that create a conflict of interest or appear to compromise their ability to make objective decisions in the best interest of Berkeley Services.
  • Fair Competition: Vendors shall compete fairly and honestly, refraining from any anti-competitive practices, price-fixing, or market manipulation.

4. Sustainability and Environmental Responsibility

Berkeley Services is committed to minimizing its environmental impact and expects its partners to actively support this commitment by:

  • Using Environmentally Preferable Products (EPP) that reduce environmental harm across the product life cycle, from production to disposal.
  • Implementing waste reduction, recycling, and responsible resource consumption practices.
  • Prioritizing energy-efficient products, equipment, and technologies that contribute to reducing carbon emissions and conserving natural resources.
  • Complying with ISO 14001 standards for Environmental Management Systems to continually improve sustainability practices.

5. Health, Safety, and Wellbeing

Vendors and contractors must ensure the health, safety, and wellbeing of their employees by:

  • Providing a safe and hazard-free working environment in compliance with UAE labour laws and international safety standards.
  • Conducting regular safety training, emergency preparedness, and risk assessments.
  • Reporting any accidents, injuries, or unsafe conditions to Berkeley Services immediately, with measures for preventing future occurrences.
  • Complying with ISO 45001 standards for Occupational Health & Safety Management Systems and other legal requirements for the relevant operations.

6. Labor Practices and Human Rights

Vendors are expected to uphold the highest labour standards by:

  • Prohibiting Child Labor: Ensuring that no child labour is employed in any part of their supply chain, in line with international labour standards.
  • Eliminating Forced Labor: Prohibiting all forms of forced, bonded, or involuntary labour practices.
  • Fair Treatment: Providing equal opportunities to all employees, without discrimination based on race, gender, age, nationality, disability, or any other protected characteristic.
  • Fair Wages and Working Conditions: Complying with all applicable wage laws, including minimum wage, overtime, and legally mandated benefits.

7. Supply Chain Sustainability

Vendors are encouraged to promote sustainability within their supply chains by:

  • Working with their suppliers to adopt sustainable sourcing practices that reduce environmental impact.
  • Ensuring transparency in supply chain operations and providing information on the origins of materials used.
  • Participating in initiatives that promote fair trade and ethical sourcing to support social and economic development.

8. Quality Assurance and Performance

Berkeley Services expects vendors to deliver products and services of the highest quality, in accordance with:

  • Industry standards and specifications outlined in contracts or purchase agreements.
  • Continuous improvement initiatives to enhance product quality, performance, and innovation.
  • Responsiveness to feedback and the prompt resolution of any quality-related issues.
  • Complying with ISO 9001 standards for Quality Management Systems.

9. Data Protection and Confidentiality

Vendors must respect the confidentiality of all proprietary information provided by Berkeley Services, ensuring that:

  • Sensitive data is securely stored and protected from unauthorized access.
  • Data protection measures comply with relevant UAE data privacy laws, including data breach notification and encryption practices.
  • Confidential information is not disclosed to third parties without the express written consent of Berkeley Services.

10. Reporting Unethical Behaviour

  • Vendors are required to report any violations of this Code or unethical behaviour by any party in their operations to Berkeley Services through the designated reporting channels.
  • Reports can be made confidentially, and there will be no retaliation against those who raise concerns in good faith.

11. Monitoring and Compliance

  • Berkeley Services reserves the right to audit and evaluate vendors' compliance with this Code and sustainability standards at any time.
  • Non-compliance with the standards outlined in this Code may result in corrective actions, contract termination, or disqualification from future business opportunities with Berkeley Services.

12. Acknowledgment and Acceptance

By doing business with Berkeley Services, all vendors and contractors acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agree to comply with this Vendor/Contractor Code of Conduct. They also agree to communicate these standards to their employees, agents, and subcontractors to ensure adherence at every level of their operations.

11. FORCE MAJEURE

11.1 Force Majeure Event means any event or condition beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, which:

  • (a) arises after the date of this Agreement;
  • (b) is beyond a Party's control;
  • (c) such Party could not reasonably have provided against before entering into this Agreement;
  • (d) having arisen, such Party could not reasonably have avoided or overcome;
  • (e) completely and totally prevents the performance of any of the Party's obligations under this Agreement;
  • (f) does not as a result of the Party's own fault or negligence; and
  • (g) is not substantially attributable to the other Party.

11.2 A Force Majeure Event shall include: (i) war, revolution, riot, terrorism or civil commotion; (ii) radiation or contamination by radioactivity from any nuclear waste or any other hazardous properties or chemicals; (iii) interruption or failure of utility service including but not limited to electricity, gas or water; (iv) natural catastrophe including but not limited to earthquakes, floods, fire, tsunamis, adverse weather or other serious epidemics; (v) criminal damage, sabotage, blockade, embargo, strike, lock out or other industrial disturbances or trade dispute; and (vi) governmental action or decision of any nature whatsoever which materially and adversely affects the legal position of a Party to continue with its obligations under this Agreement; but, for the avoidance of doubt, shall exclude (i) circumstances arising from economic downturn, exchange rate fluctuation or inability to make payment; (ii) reasonably foreseeable weather conditions; and (iii) financial condition of the SUPPLIER or any sub-SUPPLIER.

11.3 If any Force Majeure Event occurs which renders a Party unable to perform or complete any of its obligations under this Agreement and/or under any Purchase Order issued by COMPANY, the Party so affected shall immediately notify the other in writing of the occurrence of any Force Majeure Event and the cause & likely duration of the cause, delay or non-performance of its obligations under the Agreement and/or under any Purchase order issued by COMPANY.

11.4 The SUPPLIER shall only be entitled to rely on this Force Majeure Clause where it has a sufficient disaster recovery plan in place.
